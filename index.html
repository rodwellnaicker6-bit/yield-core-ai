🪵 What is Biochar — and why does it matter for SA farmers?
Biochar is a stable, carbon-rich material produced by heating biomass (crop residues, wood waste, sugarcane bagasse) in a low-oxygen process called pyrolysis. When applied to soil it locks carbon away for hundreds to thousands of years — far longer than any cover crop or composting method. It also improves water retention, raises soil pH, and boosts fertiliser uptake, making it one of the most powerful tools for SA dryland farmers.

SA Feedstocks for Biochar
Maize stalks & cobs — most abundant. Produced post-harvest from your fields.

Sugarcane bagasse — KZN & Mpumalanga. High-volume, low-ash biochar.

Invasive alien wood — Wattle, Port Jackson. Biochar is a government-supported removal pathway.

Macadamia shells & peach pits — Western Cape orchards. Premium high-carbon biochar.
🌱
Soil Benefits — SA Context
Sandy Karoo soils — Biochar dramatically improves CEC and water-holding. Drought resilience improvement of up to 40% measured in trials.

Clay soils (Highveld) — Reduces compaction, improves aeration, root penetration.

Acidic soils (KZN) — Raises pH similar to lime but lasts decades. pH shift of 0.5–1.2 units documented.

Mycorrhizal habitat — Porous structure acts as habitat for beneficial fungi — synergy with our bio-inputs.
💰
Revenue Potential per Hectare
Applying biochar at 3 t/ha sequesters approximately 2.4–3.0 tCO₂e/ha per year.

At current VCM spot prices of ~R341/t, this generates R818–R1,023 per ha annually in carbon credit revenue — on top of yield improvements from soil health gains.

Over a 10-year verified project, a 500 ha farm could earn R4.1M–R5.1M in carbon credits alone, plus yield savings from reduced fertiliser.
⚠️
Application Guidelines
Rate: 1–5 t/ha depending on crop, soil type, and budget. Start at 2 t/ha for first season.

Timing: Incorporate before planting — ideally 4–6 weeks before. Broadcast and disc-in 10–15cm deep.

Activation: Pre-charge with compost tea or liquid N-Fix Pro before application for best results.

Testing: Run baseline soil carbon test before application and annually thereafter for carbon credit verification.
🔁 Biochar Production Process
1
Collect Feedstock
Harvest crop residues, alien wood, or arrange collection with a local biochar co-op. Must be dry (<20% moisture).
2
Pyrolysis / Kiln
Heat to 400–700°C in a flame-cap kiln or continuous retort. YieldCore partner kilns available in WC, KZN, GP.
3
Quality Test
Measure pH, surface area, volatile matter. Must meet European Biochar Certificate (EBC) or IBI standards for credit eligibility.
4
Apply & Monitor
Incorporate into soil. Collect GPS-logged field photos. YieldCore auto-logs application events for verification audit trail.

🏛️ South African Carbon Tax — What Farmers Need to Know
South Africa's Carbon Tax Act (No. 15 of 2019) places a levy on greenhouse gas emissions — but it also creates a mechanism for farmers to earn offset credits that reduce carbon tax liabilities. Agriculture is exempt from direct carbon tax, but farmers can sell offsets to emitters who need to reduce their liability. Understanding this creates a genuine new revenue stream.
Carbon Tax Rate (2025)
R236/tCO₂e
Increases by CPI + 2% each year until 2030. Phase 2 (2026–2030) will expand to more sectors and close exemptions.
Max Offset Allowance
Up to 10%
Emitters can use approved carbon offsets to reduce up to 10% of their carbon tax liability. Agriculture and forestry offsets are approved.
Farmer's Offset Price
~R190–R230/t
SA Carbon Tax offsets trade at a discount to the full tax rate. Typically R190–R230/tCO₂e for SARS-approved Section 13 credits.
Approved Activities
4 Categories
Soil carbon sequestration, biochar application, agroforestry / windbreaks, improved manure management (Section 13 regulations).
📋 How to Claim Section 13 Offset Credits with SARS
1
Establish a Baseline Measurement
Commission a DEA&DP-accredited baseline soil carbon assessment before you start your sequestration activity. This is the legal starting point for any offset claim. Cost: approximately R4,000–R8,000 per farm.
2
Implement an Approved Sequestration Activity
Apply biochar, establish agroforestry buffers, convert to no-till, or implement improved grazing. Keep GPS-logged records of every application event — YieldCore auto-logs these for you.
3
Third-Party Verification
After 12 months, appoint a SANAS-accredited verifier (SGS, Bureau Veritas, or TÜV Rheinland) to audit your records and soil measurements. They issue a Verified Emission Reduction (VER) statement.
4
Register in the South African Carbon Offset Administration System (COAS)
Submit your verified VER statement to SARS / DFFE via COAS. Credits are issued as South African offset certificates linked to your entity tax number.
5
Sell Your Credits to a Taxable Emitter
Credits can be sold directly to a mining house, manufacturer, or energy company who needs to reduce their carbon tax bill — or via a broker (Absa Carbon, Standard Bank Green Finance, Carbon Neutral Group SA). Payment is in ZAR, taxable as income under the Income Tax Act.
